Post 35/15/18 10AM

Long story short... I'm scrapping this design. I like the hobbies page, its cool and all. But that's it. Enough Gifs. Enough useless content. This site is supposed to showcase who I am and the gifs are just a representation of my likes, not my character. Big picture was not the intent of this site initially. See me in 3 months when the site is fully redesigned

Countless hours have been spent in my life learning new things. Things that no one taught me to do until I "had" to learn how to do it because I was told that was the case. Things management doesn't teach you. Things no one teaches you but your own experiences. Things you learn in the real world. How to read API JSONs when you never knew what a JSON was before. Learning what is and how to use Charles Proxy, so as to determine network calls for media players, commerce flows, or geolocation and content blocking. Being able to communicate the point effectively to meet the expectations of your clients and peers. Learning a new coding language independently prevent your own obsolescence. Knowing what is IN AND OUT OF SCOPE in terms of your job, the project, the expectations, and literally anything else that is defined by some sort of requirement. Knowing your own capabilities and being able to deliver something that gives you self-worth. The time you spend with the people that drive you to be better than you were before. None of these things are taught. All things are learned through experience. I am firmly a kinesthetic learner and an advocate for immersion with those who aim to achive something bigger than the individual.

This site will continue to exist as an expression of my thoughts and feelings during the past year - maybe some pages will carry on outside of the Archive.gov webcrawler - and in the future you will see a new form that represents my ideals, values, and both personal and professional aspirations. I gave it my best, given a full-time job, a three hour commute, and a lack of both physical and mental strength. This was an experiment, first and foremost. I had no goals when I bought this domain. That said, goal line has shifted and, as a result, so have my intentions. Take care, now. Bye bye, then. End goal: less CSS, more "content". To those of you who say: "James, there's nothing here.
